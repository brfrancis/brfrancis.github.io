Value Pillar 2

Open Data Foundation

Reduce data tax across finance, actuarial and partner ecosystems

Phoenix pain
  • Phoenix has a mixed estate across AWS, Azure, Teradata, SQL Server and Databricks.
  • Databricks is used for actuarial modelling and risk analytics.
  • Data movement creates friction, duplication and governance complexity.
  • External exchange can rely on manual, insecure or stale processes.
Business driver

Phoenix needs the right teams using the right tools without every domain creating its own copy of data.

Snowflake outcome
  • Governed enterprise data foundation
  • Apache Iceberg / open interoperability
  • Databricks coexistence for actuarial workloads
  • Snowpark for Python on Snowflake warehouses
  • Secure Data Sharing and Horizon Catalog / Marketplace discoverability
  • Workload isolation and multi-cloud flexibility
Positioning

Snowflake does not need to displace Databricks where Databricks is the right actuarial modelling environment. The opportunity is to reduce data tax by making actuarial outputs governed, interoperable and consumable across Finance, Customer and Risk.

So what

Your teams can reduce repeated CSV download, transformation, loading and reconciliation — improving freshness while reducing engineering effort.

Snowpark enables Python and other code to run against data in Snowflake using Snowflake warehouses — reducing data movement and separate infrastructure.

Apache Iceberg is primarily an open table format and interoperability story, not a compression story.

Value Pillar 3

Trusted AI at Scale

Industrialise AI with governance, evidence and control

Phoenix pain
  • Phoenix wants GenAI for claims processing and personalised pension guidance.
  • Leadership is concerned about governance, auditability, data movement and complexity.
  • In retirement and claims, AI must be controlled, explainable and permission-aware.
Business driver

Phoenix needs to scale AI safely across regulated customer and operational workflows.

Snowflake outcome
  • Cortex AI and Cortex Analyst pattern, shown here in Cortex-ready / trial-safe simulation
  • Governed AI close to enterprise data
  • RBAC at query/prompt context
  • Masking and row access policies
  • Auditability and lineage
  • Human-in-the-loop operating model
So What

Same question: “Summarise this customer’s pension position and suggest next best actions.”

  • Customer Service Agent: sees servicing history and approved customer-facing information.
  • Financial Adviser / Specialist: sees contribution history, retirement gap indicators and authorised allocation data.
  • Executive / Retention Lead: sees aggregated retention risk, cohort trends and commercial insight.

The model is not the control point. The governed context is. In this trial-safe experience, the assistant journey is Cortex-ready without overclaiming live LLM invocation.
