Panel 2

Open Enterprise Interoperability

Reduce data tax without forcing every workload into one tool

Battleground

Phoenix has a mixed technology estate and must avoid creating another silo.

Phoenix Pain
  • AWS, Azure, Teradata, SQL Server and Databricks all exist in the landscape.
  • Databricks is used for actuarial modelling and risk analytics.
  • Platform sprawl creates duplication, reconciliation effort, governance inconsistency and slow insight.
  • The issue is not that Databricks exists; it is that actuarial outputs can become isolated from Finance, Customer and Risk teams.
Why It Matters
  • Enterprise transformation fails when each domain has its own data copy, logic and governance model.
  • Actuarial outputs are high value only if Finance, Risk and Customer teams can consume them without friction.
  • The opportunity is interoperability, not religious standardisation.
Financial / Operational Impact
  • Higher platform run cost
  • Duplicated data engineering
  • Reconciliation overhead
  • Slow finance and actuarial reporting cycles
  • Delayed enterprise consumption of actuarial insight
Snowflake Capability
  • Governed enterprise data foundation
  • Apache Iceberg / open table interoperability
  • Apache Polaris / open catalogue positioning where relevant
  • Snowpark for Python workloads running on Snowflake warehouses
  • Secure Data Sharing, Marketplace and Native Apps
  • Workload isolation through separated storage and compute
  • Multi-cloud SaaS deployment flexibility; SnowConvert migration awareness for Teradata
So What / Value

Snowpark enables developers to run Python and other code directly against data in Snowflake using Snowflake warehouses — without moving data into separate infrastructure. That means less data movement, governed execution close to data, elastic compute, auditability and fewer operational handoffs.

Databricks Objection Handling

“We already use Databricks for actuarial. Why Snowflake?”

I would not position Snowflake as a forced replacement for Databricks. For actuarial modelling, Databricks may remain the preferred environment. The opportunity is to make actuarial outputs interoperable with Finance, Customer and Risk through governed open data patterns such as Iceberg, reducing the data tax rather than creating another silo.

Iceberg is primarily an open interoperability and table-format story, not a compression story. Compression depends on the underlying file format and storage implementation. The Snowflake value is governed access, performance and multi-engine interoperability.

Illustrative KPIs
Data movementReduce duplicated movement between actuarial, finance and customer domains.
ReconciliationReduce cycles between modelling outputs and finance reporting.
FreshnessImprove freshness of actuarial outputs consumed downstream.
Ops burdenReduce platform handoffs and support burden.

Executive message: Interoperability does not mean standardising on one tool. It means reducing friction between the right tools.

Panel 3

Trusted AI at Scale

Industrialise AI without increasing governance risk

Battleground

Phoenix wants AI, but AI must be governed, explainable and safe.

Phoenix Pain
  • GenAI ambitions for claims automation and personalised pension guidance.
  • Leadership concern about data movement, governance, complexity and auditability.
  • In retirement and claims, unauthorised or poorly governed AI is operational and regulatory risk.
Why It Matters

The strategic challenge is no longer whether AI can generate an answer. The question is whether Phoenix can trust, govern and audit that answer. In regulated financial services, AI without provenance becomes operational risk.

Financial / Operational Impact
  • Slower AI adoption and delayed productivity gains
  • Higher AI operating risk and compliance exposure
  • Poor ROI from fragmented AI initiatives
  • Increased review burden if AI outputs cannot be explained
Snowflake Capability
  • Cortex AI and Cortex Analyst
  • Semantic models where appropriate
  • Governed data access
  • RBAC at query/prompt context
  • Masking and row access policies
  • Auditability, lineage and reduced data movement
  • Human-in-the-loop operating model
So What / Value

The model is not the control point. The governed context is. Phoenix can scale AI while preserving access control, lineage and evidence.

RBAC at Prompt Demonstration

Same question: “Summarise this customer’s pension position and suggest next best actions.”

Sees policy status, servicing history and approved customer-facing information. Does not see profitability, internal retention scoring or restricted actuarial insight.

Sees contribution history, retirement gap indicators and investment allocation where authorised.

Sees aggregated retention risk, cohort trends and commercial insight.

Illustrative KPIs
ProductivityReduce manual review time for claims or pension guidance preparation.
AuditabilityIncrease auditability of AI-assisted decisions.
MovementReduce data movement into external AI environments.
ConfidenceImprove governance confidence for AI rollout.

Executive message: AI without provenance is operational risk.

Panel 4

Collaboration & Marketplace

Fresh external data without the copy-transform-load tax

Battleground

Phoenix needs better collaboration with external partners and richer external data, but legacy exchange methods create latency, cost and risk.

Phoenix Pain
  • External data exchange relies on manual or insecure legacy processes.
  • Teams may receive CSVs, transform them, load them, reconcile them and only then make them usable.
  • By the time external data is consumed, it may already be stale.
  • Every copied dataset creates another governance, lineage and quality burden.
Why It Matters

Secure Data Sharing and Marketplace are not valuable merely because they are technical features. They are valuable because Phoenix can consume governed external data without repeatedly copying, transforming and loading it.

Illustrative example, non-Phoenix

Example from another industry: a company spending £1.5m per year on data engineering just to download, transform and ingest third-party CSV data that is already months old. With Snowflake Marketplace / Secure Data Sharing, the same class of data can be accessed far fresher, without building the same ingestion pipeline.

Financial / Operational Impact
  • Avoidable data engineering spend
  • Stale external data and manual reconciliation
  • Slower forecasting and insight
  • Increased security exposure from file-based exchange
  • Duplicated data governance effort
Snowflake Capability
  • Secure Data Sharing
  • Snowflake Marketplace
  • Live governed access to third-party data
  • No repeated CSV download / transform / ingest cycle where sharing is available
  • Fresher data for customer, investment, risk and market insight
SO WHAT?

So what: Phoenix can spend less time moving data and more time using it.

  • For Head of Pensions: Single Customer View improves engagement, retention and Consumer Duty evidence.

Objection Handling

Preferred first response: “Why do you feel it’s expensive?” Then re-centre: “Is the concern unit cost, total platform cost, migration cost, or value realisation?” Map back to workload economics, separation of storage and compute, auto-suspend / auto-scale, reduced platform sprawl, reduced duplicated engineering, operational productivity and faster time-to-value.

Great — let’s preserve where Databricks is strong for actuarial and risk modelling, while using Snowflake to govern, share and operationalise those outputs across the enterprise.

That is exactly why AI needs to be close to governed enterprise data, with RBAC, masking, lineage, semantic controls and auditability built into the operating model.

The value is not the feature. The value is removing the copy-transform-load cycle, improving data freshness and reducing external data engineering cost.
