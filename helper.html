Pillar 2 cue card

Open Data Foundation

The goal is not to force every workload into one tool. It is to reduce friction between the right tools.

Remember

  • Databricks coexistence
  • Iceberg = interoperability, not compression
  • Snowpark = Python on Snowflake warehouses
  • Secure Data Sharing = remove copy-transform-load tax
  • Marketplace = fresher external data

So what

Less data movement, less duplicated engineering, fresher data, lower operating drag.

Do not say

  • Do not attack Databricks.
  • Do not imply Snowflake replaces every system.
  • Do not make Marketplace a separate pillar.

Objection handling

“Snowflake is expensive.”

  • First: “Why do you feel it’s expensive?”
  • Ask: unit cost, platform cost, migration cost, or time-to-value?
  • Re-centre on TCO.
  • Include duplicated engineering and data movement.
  • Bring back workload isolation and value realised.

“We already have Databricks.”

Great — keep Databricks where it is strong for actuarial modelling. Snowflake helps govern, share and operationalise outputs across the enterprise.

“Why Marketplace / Secure Data Sharing?”

The value is avoiding repeated CSV download, transformation, load and reconciliation. It is fresher data with less engineering effort.

“How do we trust AI?”

By grounding AI in governed data, role-based context, semantic definitions and auditable outputs.
