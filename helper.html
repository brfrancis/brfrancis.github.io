0:00–3:00 speaker prompt

Opening context

Phoenix has already landed Snowflake for IBOR; today is about the expansion path into Group Finance, Pensions & Savings and Group IT.

  • Frame this as a customer discovery / value meeting, not an interview demo.
  • Set expectations: business outcome → proof point → production control.
  • Name the three pillars: Unified Customer Intelligence, Open Data Foundation, Trusted AI at Scale.
Avoid saying
  • “This is just a demo site.”
  • “Snowflake replaces everything Phoenix has.”
  • “Cortex is fully live in this trial.”
3:00–8:00 speaker prompt

Unified Customer Intelligence

Phoenix cannot become customer-centric if customer truth remains fragmented across policy, pension, investment and servicing data.

  • Show Customer 360 as a reusable data product, not a one-off report.
  • Use Consumer Duty Insight to connect governance with customer outcomes.
  • Position SQL links as technical proof points; do not linger unless asked.
Must say

The Streamlit workbench is the business end-user experience; the SQL workspaces are the proof points behind it.

8:00–14:00 speaker prompt

Open Data Foundation

The goal is not to force every workload into one tool. It is to reduce friction between the right tools.

  • Teradata migration: phased migration and modernisation, with SnowConvert consideration where it helps.
  • Databricks interoperability: keep actuarial modelling where appropriate, then govern and reuse outputs through Snowflake.
  • Horizon Catalog / Marketplace: discoverability and enrichment, not the primary Teradata migration mechanism.
Avoid saying
  • “Instant lift-and-shift.”
  • “Marketplace migrates Teradata.”
  • “Databricks has to be displaced.”
14:00–22:00 speaker prompt

Trusted AI and Streamlit walkthrough

The model is not the control point. The governed context is.

  • Walk the Phoenix Customer Outcomes Workbench as the customer-facing journey.
  • Explain RBAC at prompt: same question, different permitted context by role.
  • Use Cortex-ready / trial-safe language for the pension guidance assistant.
Must say

This is Cortex-ready and trial-safe: it demonstrates the governed AI journey without overclaiming live LLM invocation in a constrained trial environment.

22:00–26:00 speaker prompt

Production controls

To move from impressive demo to production trust, Phoenix needs semantic definitions, trusted metrics and an AI audit trail.

  • Semantic layer / trusted definitions: make business meaning consistent across teams.
  • Trusted metrics: keep executive decisioning aligned to governed calculations.
  • AI audit trail: capture prompt context, outputs and review posture for regulated workflows.
26:00–29:00 speaker prompt

CoCo future initiative planning

CoCo helps turn this from a single demo into a governed roadmap for next-best-conversation assistance.

  • Position CoCo as future initiative planning, not a live production claim.
  • Connect planning to retention, retirement readiness, service quality and control evidence.
  • Keep final minute for the close.

Compact objection handling

“Why Snowflake if they already have Databricks?”

Keep Databricks for specialist actuarial modelling where it fits. Snowflake becomes the governed enterprise foundation that operationalises those outputs across analytics, finance, customer intelligence and AI.

“Why migrate from Teradata?”

Not a reckless big bang. Start with workload assessment, SnowConvert consideration, phased modernisation and value-led domain migration where elasticity, governance and data sharing reduce long-term data tax.

“Is this just a dashboard?”

No. The Streamlit view is the business experience on top of governed data products, semantic definitions, access controls and auditability.

“Is the AI actually governed?”

Yes in the pattern: RBAC, masking, row access, semantic context, audit trail and human-in-the-loop controls define what AI can see and how outputs are reviewed.

“What about trial limitations with Cortex?”

Be transparent: the experience is Cortex-ready / trial-safe. It demonstrates the production journey without pretending the trial is invoking every Cortex capability live.
